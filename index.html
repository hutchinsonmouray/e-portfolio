My Perspective

Career Goals and Passions

My career goal is to integrate my passion for developing technology to create scalable projects that positively impact education, infrastructure, or security. I am particularly interesting in EdTech and Cloud Computing. I am driven to lead initiatives that streamline processes, enhance accessibility, and improve overall efficiency. Cultural competency and an understanding of historical contexts are integral to my development approach, shaped by lessons learned during internships in South Africa and Ghana. These experiences underscored the importance of listening before acting and validating assumptions in diverse environments. I am committed to incorporating these insights into my work, ensuring that technological innovations are both contextually sensitive and globally impactful.

General Philosophy

In both professional endeavors and personal pursuits, I adhere to a philosophy anchored in continuous learning, collaboration, and empathy. I believe in fostering an environment where innovation thrives through interdisciplinary collaboration and a deep understanding of user perspectives. My research at the intersection of African American Studies and Computer Science is anchored by this. It drives my approach to design and leadership, emphasizing the importance of crafting solutions that address real-world challenges with sensitivity and inclusivity. By prioritizing user feedback, I aim to create scalable projects that not only meet immediate needs but also anticipate future advancements, contributing to sustainable progress in education, infrastructure, or security domains.

Projects

The YIT Learning Management System project comprised of developing a MERN Stack application that can be used to store information about the stakeholders in YIT's tutoring programs. This included simplfiying tasks such as measuring student attendance, tracking number of hours tutored, recording the work covered during a tutoring session, posting grades achieved by learners in the program as well as termly questionnaires to assess the effectiveness of the program.


We were able to develop an application that could:

● Host survey
